Pioneer

Through trial and failure, the lessons learned from the Pioneer satellite missions immensely aided NASA in developing its future satellite programmes

Nimbus

The Nimbus satellites signalled a revolution in weather forecasting, as they, for example, could achieve accurate forecasts over the long term. They also provided important measurements related to the Earth's environment, such as sea measurements, data on plant life in our planet's oceans, and the ozone layer. This provided vital data for assessing the effects of climate change.

Advanced TIROS - N

The final instalment of TIROS series introduced bigger slot for the apparatus and better ways of data transmission, despite keeping the main basic blueprint. Newer versions of the sensors to measure Earth radiation budget and SBUR, used to investigate ozone profiles, allowed us to see the better picture of Earth’s climate

ATS

The purpose of the Application Technology Satellite program was to study and in-flight test technological developments common to many satellite applications. The spacecraft performed various communications, meteorology, and science experiments in addition to evaluating three types of spacecraft stabilization systems.

LGS

The program consisting of two satellites, which by themselves have no sensors or even electronics, have to relay on ground stations to accept and transit back laser impulses. This simple and elegant system design allows, however, high precision reference measurements of objects on Earth.

TIROS

The main achievement of the original TIROS experimental program was to prove the liability of the satellites as Earth study instruments. The followed up TIROSs as well as other satellites programs heavily relied on developments first implemented here. Understanding of Earth’s radiation budget allowed to further showcase the Global Climate Change reality

TOS

Using sensors originally created for Nimbus Project, the upgraded TIROSs used in TIROS Operational System allowed better understanding of the cloud pattern formation and furthered our knowledge in meteorology

ITOS

TIROS-M/ITOS, built in collaboration in research between NASA and NOAA, this program allowed installation of the new generation of the sensors which helped satellites on their sun-synchronous orbit to produce best data to help observation and weather forecast

TIROS-N

This program continued tradition of NASA and NOAA cooperation. The global cloud cover now could be observed 24/7 with getting into nuances of the atmospheric water and temperature profiles. The study of particle flux on near Earth orbit helped to understand what dangers can organisms encounter on the orbital stations

GOES

Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellite is a joint programme of NASA and the NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration) - these spacecraft help meteorologists observe and predict local weather events, as well as natural disasters, which has been instrumental in protecting human life on Earth, as through previewing these events less people are affected by them.

Landsat

The satellites in the Landsat series have recorded data which continues to be used by decision makers today, such as data on the forests, farms and cities of the Earth. For instance, Landsat data is used to better understand climate change.
